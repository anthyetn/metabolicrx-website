What is Low Testosterone?

Low testosterone (Low T or hypogonadism) occurs when your body doesn't produce enough of this vital hormone. Testosterone levels naturally decline with age, typically starting around age 30, but some men experience more significant drops that affect their quality of life.

Testosterone plays a crucial role in maintaining muscle mass, bone density, red blood cell production, sex drive, sperm production, and overall mood and energy levels.

When levels fall below normal ranges (typically 300-1000 ng/dL), you may experience a variety of symptoms that impact your physical health, mental well-being, and quality of life.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is a good candidate for testosterone therapy? +
Men who are experiencing symptoms of low testosterone and have confirmed low T levels through blood testing are typically good candidates. This usually includes men over 30 with testosterone levels below 300 ng/dL, though individual circumstances vary. Your provider will evaluate your complete health profile to determine if TRT is appropriate for you.
How quickly will I see results from TRT? +
Most men begin noticing improvements in energy, mood, and libido within 3-6 weeks of starting treatment. Physical changes like increased muscle mass and reduced body fat typically become noticeable within 3-6 months. Maximum benefits are usually achieved within 6-12 months of consistent treatment.
Is testosterone replacement therapy safe? +
When properly monitored by qualified healthcare providers, TRT is generally safe for most men. Your Metabolic Rx provider will conduct regular lab tests and check-ins to monitor your response and adjust treatment as needed. We carefully screen for contraindications and monitor for potential side effects to ensure your safety throughout treatment.
What are the potential side effects? +
Possible side effects may include acne, fluid retention, increased red blood cell count, breast enlargement, testicular shrinkage, and decreased sperm production. However, many of these can be managed or prevented with proper monitoring and dose adjustments. Your provider will discuss all potential risks and benefits during your consultation.
Do I need to be on TRT forever? +
The duration of TRT varies by individual. Some men choose to continue indefinitely to maintain benefits, while others may use it for a specific period. If you stop treatment, your testosterone levels will gradually return to their pre-treatment baseline. Your provider will help you make informed decisions about the duration of your treatment based on your goals and response.
Will TRT affect my fertility? +
TRT can reduce sperm production and may affect fertility while you're taking it. If you're planning to have children, discuss this with your provider before starting treatment. Alternative therapies or protocols that preserve fertility may be available. In many cases, fertility returns after discontinuing TRT, though this isn't guaranteed.
How often will I need lab work? +
Initially, we typically check your levels after 6-8 weeks to ensure your dosage is optimized. Once stabilized, we recommend lab work every 3-6 months to monitor testosterone levels, red blood cell count, estradiol, and other important markers. Regular monitoring ensures both safety and effectiveness of your treatment.
